Ohms Law

Ohm's law describes the relationship between Voltage(V) Current(I) and Resistance(R) within a circuit. The law can be expressed as the equation V=IR and can be used to find a missing variable.

This demonstration shows Ohm's law using a simple circuit with a single power supply and resistor. In the diagram the Voltage is marked in blue, the current is marked in red and the resistance is marked in green.
